AUTUMN: singapore

Bringing out the autumn vibes at the sunnyside

During my school term break, I had a stopgap weekend all to myself, as I found some unpleasant news. I decided to go out on a journey to capture the beautiful landscapes of Singapore in warmth, earthy tones, capturing scenes that was almsot a juxstaposition to my feelings. I also wanted to capture somehow, the poignancy during the transition of turning over a new leaf, a new life.

jupiter frontier: cambodia

Captured photos of my month mission trip

During my November to December school break, after a month of planning and contacting villages and schools, I decided to lay everything down to travel to Cambodia to volunteer as an english teacher, teaching children in a village called Jiro. I was also given the opportunity to give a presentation talk to the other teachers there on how to teach in an effective way since I have been teaching for four years in and out of school. I also got to document and interview a few people in a school called Goldstone school of HOPE Worldwide. This experience really softened my heart to be more generous with my time, as getting to know people's stories changed my life - and also to learn to bring light to the dark because all of us exist to make a difference in people's lives.

More thoughts, feelings and photographs on my personal blog.

FIRST: AUSSIE

My first getaway trip

Around Melbourne and Sydney - in this trip I attended the School of Missions church history programme that lasted from morning to evening for three days straight, and then a retreat that consisted of people from the SPA region, freezing my butt off in a cabin without a blanket on top of the blue mountains, but met the most amazing human beings. I fell in love with this country because the hues of the skies, the trees, the mountains, the sun; they were all saturated with vibrance.

COLD HOME: KOREA

poignant journey to my winter home

During my winter break, I took an abrupt trip home as I found some unsound news about my beloved grandmother who was on the verge of something unknown - preparing my heart to make sure I don't falter into destitude or any form of despondency. This is my journey of taking pain and looking through another point of view, making things around me beautiful. As I faced this perfectly alone, I did not want to spiral down into my own world and forget the ubiquitous for my selfish ambitions. Instead, from pain, to gain the touch of the world. This was how I spend my days losing the world but gaining everything else instead without losing myself.
